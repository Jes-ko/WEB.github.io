Habitat
destruction

Definition

Sri Lanka's coastal areas are experiencing rapid development, leading to the destruction and alteration of nesting beaches. The construction of hotels, resorts, and other coastal infrastructure can destroy nesting sites and disrupt the natural habitat of turtles. Additionally, deforestation of coastal vegetation removes the natural barriers that protect nesting beaches from erosion and human disturbances.

Illegal trade

Definition

Illegal trade poses a significant threat to turtles in Sri Lanka. Turtles and their eggs are captured and traded for various purposes, including their meat, shells, and eggs. Turtle meat is considered a delicacy in some regions, and their shells are used for traditional crafts and souvenirs. The demand for these products drives the illegal harvesting of turtles and their eggs, which leads to population declines and hampers conservation efforts.

Climate change

Definition

The impacts of climate change pose challenges for turtle populations. Rising temperatures can affect the nesting success of turtles. For example, warmer sands can influence the gender ratio of hatchlings, as the incubation temperature determines the sex of the offspring. Higher temperatures may result in more females, leading to an imbalance in populations. Additionally, sea level rise and changes in ocean currents can erode nesting beaches and disrupt nesting patterns.

Bycatch

Definitio

Turtles often become unintentional victims of fishing activities. They can get entangled in fishing gear, such as nets, lines, and hooks. When unable to surface for air, turtles can drown. Bottom trawling, a fishing method that involves dragging a net along the seafloor, is particularly harmful to turtles, as they can get trapped and injured. Bycatch is a significant threat to turtle populations, as it can lead to injury or death.

Pollution

Definition

Pollution, especially plastic debris, poses a severe threat to turtles in Sri Lanka. Turtles can mistake plastic bags, bottles, and other litter for food and ingest them, causing internal injuries, blockages, and death. Coastal areas and the ocean are also impacted by oil spills, chemical pollution, and agricultural runoff, which can contaminate the water and affect the health of turtles and their habitats.

Predation

Definition

Natural predators pose a threat to turtle nests and eggs. Crabs, birds, raccoons, and other animals may prey on turtle eggs or disturb nesting sites. Predation rates can be particularly high in areas with reduced natural predator populations due to human activities, as the absence of these predators can disrupt the natural balance and increase predation pressure on turtle nests. Additionally, feral dogs and cats, which are present in some coastal areas, can disturb nesting sites and prey on hatchlings.
