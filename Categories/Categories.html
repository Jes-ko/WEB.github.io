Olive Ridley Turtles

Olive Ridley turtles are a species of sea turtles that can be found in Sri Lanka. They are known for their unique nesting behavior, where thousands of females come ashore simultaneously to lay their eggs. Sri Lanka's coastline serves as a significant nesting site for Olive Ridley turtles, particularly along the southern and eastern regions. Conservation efforts are in place to protect their nesting habitats, as they are considered vulnerable due to factors like habitat destruction and accidental capture in fishing nets.

Green Turtles

Green turtles, scientifically known as Chelonia mydas, are another species of sea turtles found in Sri Lanka. They are named for their greenish-colored fat and skin. Green turtles are known to nest on Sri Lanka's beaches, with popular nesting sites including Rekawa, Kosgoda, and Pigeon Island. These turtles undertake long-distance migrations and are known to travel thousands of kilometers between feeding and nesting grounds.

Hawksbill Turtles

Hawksbill turtles (Eretmochelys imbricata) can also be found in Sri Lanka. These turtles are known for their beautiful, patterned shells and are critically endangered. Sri Lanka's coastal waters provide important foraging grounds for hawksbill turtles, and they also nest on several beaches around the island, including Rekawa, Kosgoda, and Rumassala. Hawksbill turtles face numerous threats, including habitat loss, pollution, and illegal hunting for their shells.

Leatherback Turtles

Leatherback turtles (Dermochelys coriacea) are an endangered species of sea turtles that can occasionally be found in Sri Lanka. However, their presence in Sri Lankan waters and nesting sites is relatively rare compared to other turtle species. Leatherbacks are the largest of all sea turtles and are known for their unique leathery shells. They undertake long-distance migrations and can travel across oceans.

Loggerhead Turtles

Loggerhead turtles (Caretta caretta) are not typically found in Sri Lanka. While loggerhead turtles have a wide distribution and can be found in various oceans, their nesting activity is more commonly associated with regions such as the Mediterranean Sea, the North Atlantic Ocean, and the southeastern coast of the United States. Sri Lanka's beaches are primarily nesting grounds for other turtle species such as Olive Ridley, Green, and Hawksbill turtles. It is important to focus conservation efforts on protecting the local turtle populations that are native to Sri Lanka.
